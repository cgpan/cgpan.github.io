I am a third-year PhD student in the Measurement, Evaluation, and Statistics program at Teachers College Columbia University, advised by Professor Suk, Youmi. My research interest includes causal inference, causal reinforcement learning, Targeted Maximum Likelihood Estimation (TMLE), Dynamic Treatment Regimes(DTR), and algorithmic fairness. 

My current research focuses on developing personalized, data-driven recommendation models for high school math course-taking plans using DTR methods. This project is founded by Prof. Suk's NSF funding and in collaboration with Yuxuan Li. By building doubly robust, and asymptotically efficient estimators using the TMLE method, we examine the performance of TMLE-based DTRs compared to all other DTR methods (Causal Forest, Bart, etc.) in longitudinal settings with multicategorical treatments.
